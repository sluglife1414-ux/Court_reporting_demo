KB-001 | OBJECTION FORMAT — LOUISIANA CORRECTION
Priority: HIGH | Overrides: State module default objection normalization
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PROBLEM: State module instructed engine to normalize all objections to
"Objection, form." This is WRONG. The engine changed "Objection form." to
"Objection, form." when the correct answer is "Objection to form."

RULE: In Louisiana depositions, the standard objection to form is:
  CORRECT:   "Objection to form."
  CORRECT:   "Objection to form; vague."  (semicolon between bases)
  INCORRECT: "Objection, form."            ← do NOT use this format

When an objection has multiple bases, separate with a semicolon, not a comma.
  Example: "Objection to form; vague."
  Example: "Objection; mischaracterizes the document."

DO NOT collapse "Objection to form, vague." to "Objection, form." or any
shorter form. Preserve the attorney's stated objection bases.

WHAT TO CORRECT: Only correct obvious transcription errors in objections
(e.g., missing period, clear typo in attorney name). Do not normalize wording.

CONFIRMED IN: LA_raw_01_key.json — objections category, score 0/1 this run.

  - "Certified Court Reporter in and for the State of Louisiana" line
  - Explicit method of reporting statement (e.g., "reported in stenotype")

LOUISIANA CERTIFICATION MUST CONTAIN ALL OF THE FOLLOWING:
  1. Reporter's full name
  2. Title: "Certified Court Reporter"
  3. "in and for the State of Louisiana"  ← often missing, must be present
  4. Statement that testimony was taken in person

  6. Statement that it was transcribed by reporter
  7. Truth and correctness statement
  8. Non-relation statement (not related to counsel or parties)
  9. Non-interest statement (not interested in outcome)
 10. Location and date: "Baton Rouge, Louisiana, this ___ day of ___, 20__."
 11. Signature line
 12. Reporter name printed below signature
 13. CCR designation: "CCR" or "CERTIFIED COURT REPORTER"
 14. License number: "LOUISIANA CERTIFIED COURT REPORTER NO. XXXX"

FLAG any missing element individually. Do not collapse to a single flag.

KB-005 | DO NOT HYPHENATE — "ONSITE"
Priority: MEDIUM | Category: hyphenation / overcorrection prevention
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PROBLEM: Engine changed "onsite" → "on-site." This is an overcorrection.
In petroleum engineering and oilfield operations context, "onsite" is
accepted as a single unhyphenated word.

RULE: "onsite" — keep as one word. Do NOT change to "on-site."

Similarly accepted as single words in oilfield/engineering context:
  onsite      (not on-site)
  offsite     (not off-site)
  offshore    (not off-shore)
  onshore     (not on-shore)
  downhole    (not down-hole)
  wellbore    (not well-bore)
  wellhead    (not well-head)

These are compound nouns, not compound modifiers — do not hyphenate.

KB-006 | CASING PHYSICS — DOMAIN KNOWLEDGE RULE
Priority: HIGH | Category: super_rare_domain | Flag type: CRITICAL DOMAIN FLAG
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
NOTE: This rule is valid domain knowledge even though the scoring of this
specific item in L3-001 is disputed (key may contain an error — see SCORES_LOG).

RULE: In oil and gas well construction, casing strings must decrease in
diameter from the outermost to the innermost. The required order from
LARGEST to SMALLEST is:

  Conductor casing  (largest — set at shallowest depth, e.g., ~100 ft)
  Surface casing    (e.g., 13-3/8", set at ~2,500 ft)
  Intermediate casing  (e.g., 11-3/4", set at ~9,800 ft)
  Production casing    (e.g., 9-5/8", set at total depth)

If any transcript lists casing sizes out of this physical order
(e.g., intermediate smaller than production, or production larger than
surface), FLAG IMMEDIATELY:
  [FLAG: CRITICAL — CASING SIZE PHYSICALLY IMPOSSIBLE. Intermediate casing
   (X") cannot be smaller than production casing (Y"). Verify testimony
   and exhibits. Possible transcription error in size or casing type.]

WHY THIS MATTERS: If a witness misstates a casing size, it goes undetected
by ordinary proofreading. This is a domain-knowledge trap that only catches
if the engine knows the physics.

CONFIRMED AS RULE: Yes. Confirmed as valid petroleum engineering physics.
Confirmed as planted test target: Disputed — see SCORES_LOG L3-001 KEY ISSUE.

KB-010 | VERBATIM RULE — DO NOT CORRECT WITNESS'S SPOKEN ERRORS
Priority: CRITICAL | Overrides: Layer 7 Terminology Engine for witness speech
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FAILURE: Agent changed "Intercoastal" → "Intracoastal" in witness testimony.
The witness SAID "Intercoastal." That is the sworn record. The agent applied
the Layer 7 terminology engine (geographic term, HIGH confidence → correct
silently) to the witness's own spoken word. This is a verbatim violation.

THE FUNDAMENTAL DISTINCTION:
  Steno/transcription error  → The machine got it wrong. FIX IT.
                               Example: "thier" → "their"
  Witness spoke it wrong     → That IS the record. DO NOT TOUCH IT.
                               Example: Witness says "Intercoastal" → transcribe
                               "Intercoastal" exactly as spoken.

RULE: Layer 7 (Terminology Engine) applies ONLY to clear steno transcription
errors — where the stenographer's machine or fingers produced the wrong word.
It does NOT apply to words the witness actually spoke, even if those words
are factually incorrect, geographically wrong, or imprecise.

WHY THIS MATTERS: In litigation, a witness's misstatement of a location, term,
or fact is itself evidence. Counsel may use it in cross-examination to challenge
the witness's knowledge or credibility. A reporter who "fixes" that word has
altered sworn testimony and potentially protected or harmed a party.

CORRECT ACTION when witness uses a wrong/imprecise geographic or factual term:
  WRONG:   Change "Intercoastal" → "Intracoastal" (alters the record)
  CORRECT: Transcribe "Intercoastal" exactly as spoken.
  OPTIONAL: If flagging adds value → [REVIEW: witness stated "Intercoastal" —
            possible reference to Intracoastal Waterway. Transcribed verbatim.]
            But DO NOT alter the word itself.

CONFIRMED IN: L1-001 audit — "smoking gun" failure per veteran auditor.
"By fixing it, the reporter accidentally protected the witness and
overstepped their role." — Auditor verdict.

KB-011 | ABRUPT TRANSCRIPT ENDINGS — PROCEDURAL PARENTHETICAL REQUIRED
Priority: HIGH | Category: formatting, record transitions
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
FAILURE: Agent correctly flagged that transcript ended mid-examination but
did not insert a procedural parenthetical to close the record.

RULE: When a transcript ends without a closing colloquy, a procedural
parenthetical must be inserted at the point of cutoff IN ADDITION to any
meta-flag. Choose the appropriate form based on context:

  Examination interrupted, off-record discussion likely:
    (Discussion held off the record.)

  Proceedings ended or adjourned:
    (Whereupon, the proceedings were adjourned.)

  Context unclear:
    (Whereupon, the proceedings were concluded.)

The meta-flag [FLAG: TRANSCRIPT ENDS MID-EXAMINATION] tells the reviewer
something is missing. The parenthetical tells THE RECORD what happened.
Both are required. One does not replace the other.

CORRECT FORMAT (at point of cutoff):
  Q.  Thank you.  Now, regarding the habendum clause in Paragraph 4 —
  (Whereupon, the proceedings were adjourned.)
  [FLAG: TRANSCRIPT ENDS MID-EXAMINATION — ...]

CONFIRMED IN: L1-001 audit — "a vet would expect a parenthetical note
rather than just a meta-flag." — Auditor verdict.
