# How the Engine Works
### For Court Reporters, Business Partners, and New Clients

---

## The Simple Version

**We do exactly what you do. We just do it in layers of precision.**

You have spent years building a system — your style, your instincts, your standards. You know how a deposition is supposed to read. You know what the steno machine gets wrong. You know when to fix it and when to leave it alone.

We did not replace that. We built a machine that works the same way you do — layer by layer, starting with universal rules and ending with *your* rules. By the time the transcript reaches you, the engine has already done what you would have done. Your job becomes review, not recovery.

## What This Means for You

**Before the engine:** You get a rough draft from the steno machine. You clean it. You format it. You check every name, every number, every exhibit reference. That is hours of work per transcript.

**After the engine:** You get a pre-cleaned transcript that has already applied your style, your state's format, and universal grammar rules. You review, spot-check, and sign it. The heavy lifting is done.

The engine does not guess. When it is not certain, it flags the line and leaves it for you. You are still the court reporter. Your name still goes on the transcript. We just make sure you are not starting from a blank page.

## How Layer 3 Gets Built (New Client Onboarding)

When you come on as a new client, here is what we need:

| What | Why |
|------|-----|
| One finished transcript you have already delivered | This is the gold standard — it shows us exactly how you work |
| Our 26-question style questionnaire | Catches details the sample might not show |
| Your practice area and primary state | Sets Layer 2 |
| Any technical dictionaries or word lists you use | Medical terms, engineering terms, legal terms specific to your work |

From those four things, we build your Layer 3 profile. Every run after that, the engine is calibrated to you specifically. Every correction you make to our output feeds back into your profile and makes the next run better.

By the third or fourth deposition, the engine looks like you. That is the goal.

---

## The Hierarchy — When Rules Conflict

When there is a conflict between layers, the lower layer always wins:

```
Layer 3 (Your style)  ← ALWAYS WINS
     overrides
Layer 2 (Your state)
     overrides
Layer 1 (Universal grammar)
```

If Gregg says one thing and you do it a different way — your way wins. Every time. The engine is not here to teach you grammar. It is here to do your job the way you would do it.

---

## The One Rule That Governs Everything

> **The witness's words are never touched.**

If the witness said it under oath, that is the sworn record. Even if it is grammatically wrong. Even if it is factually incorrect. Even if the steno machine almost certainly misheard it.

If we are not certain it is a steno error — we flag it. You decide.

That is not a feature. That is the law. And it is the most important thing the engine knows.
