SUBJECT MATTER OVERVIEW
-----------------------
This deposition arises from litigation involving the Sulphur Mines salt dome, Calcasieu
Parish, Louisiana. The plaintiffs (Yellow Rock, LLC and related entities) claim damages
arising from operations at or near the salt dome. Defendants include Westlake US 2 LLC
(f/k/a Eagle US 2 LLC), a salt cavern operator, and numerous insurance company defendants.

The testimony centers on the oil and gas exploration and production activities of White Top
Energy, LLC (operated by the witness and related parties) on the Sulphur Mines Field, and
on Yellow Rock, LLC's role as working interest owner. Key subjects include: the geological
and geophysical characterization of the Sulphur Mines salt dome; DNR permitting for
directional drilling near salt cavern operations; interaction with cavern operators Boardwalk
and Westlake; financial history and restructuring of White Top Energy, LLC; and the
chronology of management, investor, and production events from approximately 2018 through 2023.

The litigation also involves a complex web of insurance coverage defendants and issues of
successor-in-interest liability related to Westlake/Eagle entities.

KEY TESTIMONY POINTS
--------------------
1. COMPANY FORMATION AND STRUCTURE: Witness described formation of White Top Oil and Gas
   and its role as operator on the Sulphur Mines Field. White Top and Yellow Rock were
   related entities; witness served as SVP of both. Revenant Signal Energy was a minority
   LLC member. Robin French was co-manager of White Top along with Easley.

2. DNR PERMITTING AND CAVERN PROXIMITY: Extensive testimony regarding the two-step Louisiana
   DNR permitting process for drilling near salt caverns (Lafayette regional office to Baton
   Rouge IMD). IMD required no technical objections from cavern operators (Boardwalk/Westlake).
   Witness described a years-long permitting challenge, noting that Westlake's own injection
   well permit had been pending approximately three years. Yellow Rock maintained a direct
   communication protocol with cavern operators spanning three years.

3. FINANCIAL DISTRESS AND COVID-19 IMPACT: White Top Energy experienced severe financial
   distress beginning in 2020, driven by COVID-19 and the oil price crash. Production
   fell to approximately 409 bbl/day in early 2020 and approximately 70 bbl/day by December
   2021. White Top was unable to repay debt to Sunny Solar by the January 2021 maturity date.
   Monthly expenses (~$213K) exceeded monthly income (~$175K) as of late 2020.

4. GEOSCIENCE TEAM CURTAILMENT: The geoscience team (William Green, Clint Hipke, Eva Borrego)
   was furloughed, with curtailment occurring over the 12 months prior to June 2021.
   Easley's June 2021 email to Matt Steele (Exhibit 136) identified the furloughed team members.
   Borrego was later brought back as a favor to complete additional mapping work (Exhibits 154–156).

5. SUNNY SOLAR AND OWNERSHIP TRANSITION: Sunny Solar acquired a majority/controlling interest
   in White Top Energy. The August 2021 memo (Exhibit 132) announced this transition and
   named Jonathan Barrett as senior executive. By January 2023, Sunny Solar held approximately
   87% of Series A-2 equity (Exhibit 140). Witness remained on the Board of Yellow Rock
   after the Sunny Solar transition. [NOTE: "Sunny Solar" entity name to be verified against exhibits.]

6. RIGHTS OFFERING AND INVESTOR DISPUTE: A rights offering to existing LLC members was
   conducted in 2022–2023 (Exhibits 139–141). Jonathan Barrett's December 2021 email
   (Exhibit 138) disclosed approximately $500,000 in payroll taxes withheld but not remitted

7. DRILLING INVENTORY AND BRIGHT SPOT ANALYSIS: Witness testified about a ranked drilling
