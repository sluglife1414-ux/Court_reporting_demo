TOPIC 2: WHITE TOP ENERGY / YELLOW ROCK STRUCTURE AND ROLES
================================================================================

- North Summit Energy LLC was the starting entity (2015–2016); evolved into White Top
  Oil and Gas and then White Top Energy
- White Top Oil and Gas formed specifically to conduct 3D seismic survey at Sulphur Mines
- White Top Energy is the parent company; owns 100% of Yellow Rock LLC
  (as of Easley's departure, and believed still)
- Yellow Rock LLC's sole business purpose: drilling for oil and gas at Sulphur Mines Field
- Easley's role: Senior Vice President and Manager at both White Top Energy and Yellow Rock LLC
- Robin French (L.R. French, III) was Easley's boss and co-manager; they worked side by side
- Easley's responsibilities: legal, administrative, land activities, oversight of 3D seismic
  survey shoot, dealing with drilling contractors, engineering and production oversight
- Involved in decisions about which wells to drill; reviewed recommendations from
  geologists, geophysicists, and engineers
- Reviewed/prepared materials sent to investors and potential investors
- Communicated with DNR (Louisiana Dept. of Natural Resources) about permitting,
  through the company's regulatory consultant
- Had email addresses at Yellow Rock, White Top, North Summit, and North Wind LLC domains
- North Wind LLC: earlier company (out of business ~8–10 years); French and Easley
  involved; business purpose was raising capital to drill salt domes in South Louisiana
  (particularly Plaquemines Parish)
- Typical manager activities per company operating agreement: any and all company activities

================================================================================
TOPIC 3: TIMELINE — INVOLVEMENT AND DEPARTURE
================================================================================

- Late 2015: North Summit began reviewing salt dome development opportunities in South Louisiana
- 2016: intensified focus; from mid-2016 onward, 100% of Easley's time on Sulphur Mines Field
- 2017: further intensification
- Summer 2021: Easley and French replaced as White Top managers by Jonathan Barrett
  - Reason: Sunny Solar (senior secured lender), overseen by Barrett, became uncomfortable
    with the loan and determined that if they bore the financial risk going forward, they
    needed to be in control
- Fall 2021: Easley's full-time involvement ended
- ~Fall 2022: Easley completed approximately one additional year of transition/support work
  for Yellow Rock and White Top Energy
- After transition: Easley became a farmer

Post-departure: Easley remained on the Board of Yellow Rock and White Top Energy
after leaving the manager role.

TOPIC 4: SULPHUR MINES FIELD — DRILLING PROGRAM
================================================================================

- Sulphur Mines Field: the exclusive focus of Yellow Rock/White Top
- Drilling program involved both straight-hole vertical wells and directional drilling
- Well locations determined by X and Y coordinates with input from geoscience team
- The first five wells identified as critical: Fee 1024, Fee 953, Fee 954, and Fee 961
  (note: source lists four well numbers in a "first five" context; reporter to verify)
- Drilling order labeled A through V (not "A through very" — steno error corrected)
- Directional drilling required accounting for the angle (not "ankle") of the drill,
  including how sharply the drill can be directed and where the drill starts
- Anti-collision software (steno: "anti conflict") required for wellbore planning;
  FLAG: reporter to verify exact industry term used
- Drill pipe getting stuck in salt formations is a known hazard; adds to costs
- Easley concerned about penetrating the caprock (steno: "CAP bra")
- IMD (Injection and Mining Division) approval required as a first step
- Call with IMD Mines and Minerals documented in record
