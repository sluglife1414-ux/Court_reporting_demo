KB-NY-001 | SWORN vs AFFIRMED — CRITICAL VERBATIM RULE
Priority: CRITICAL | Overrides: verbatim instinct for oath language
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PROBLEM: Engine wrote "Having been first duly sworn" — AD's finished transcript
says "Having been first duly affirmed." Verbatim rule incorrectly blocked fix
because steno said "sworn" and AI treated it as spoken testimony.

CRITICAL DISTINCTION — READ THIS BEFORE APPLYING VERBATIM RULE:
  Oath language ("Having been first duly sworn/affirmed...") is REPORTER
  BOILERPLATE. It is NOT witness testimony. It is NOT something any witness
  said under oath. It is language the court reporter writes to certify how
  the witness was placed under obligation before testifying.

  The verbatim rule applies to WITNESS SPEECH only.
  The verbatim rule does NOT apply to reporter boilerplate.
  Oath language is reporter's responsibility — correct it per reporter's practice.

RULE: For AD D'Alotto NY WC depositions, ALWAYS use "affirmed":
  CORRECT:   "Having been first duly affirmed before a Notary Public..."
  INCORRECT: "Having been first duly sworn before a Notary Public..."

  - If steno says "sworn" in the oath block → CORRECT to "affirmed". Not a verbatim violation.
  - If steno says "affirmed" → keep as-is.
  - This applies everywhere the oath appears: appearances section, oath block, certificate.
  - Do NOT insert [REVIEW] — this is a known rule, not an ambiguous case.

KB-NY-002 | DOUBLE DASH — NOT EM DASH
Priority: HIGH | Overrides: master engine default em dash rule
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PROBLEM: Engine used em dash (—) for interruptions and trailing speech.
AD's finished transcript uses double dash (--) instead.

RULE: In AD D'Alotto NY WC depositions, use double dash (--) NOT em dash (—).
  CORRECT:   "I was -- I don't recall."
  INCORRECT: "I was — I don't recall."

This overrides the master engine's default em dash behavior for this reporter.
Apply to: interrupted speech, mid-sentence breaks WHERE A DASH IS WARRANTED.

IMPORTANT — DO NOT SUBSTITUTE DASHES FOR MISSING CONTENT:
  If the steno is unclear or a word is missing, do NOT insert "--" as a
  placeholder. Either reconstruct the word from context (HIGH confidence) or
  insert [REVIEW: word unclear]. A dash is punctuation, not a gap filler.
  AD uses commas far more than dashes for pauses and continuations.
  When in doubt between a comma and a dash — use a comma.
