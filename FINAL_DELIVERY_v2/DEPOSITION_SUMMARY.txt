I.   CASE OVERVIEW
================================================================================

This is a complex oil and gas litigation arising out of operations at and
around the Sulphur Mines salt dome in Calcasieu Parish, Louisiana.  Plaintiffs
-- Yellow Rock, LLC and related entities -- allege that Westlake's mismanage-
ment of its salt cavern operations caused a pressure loss event (reported loss
of pressure in Westlake's Cavern 6 or 7, beginning in or around December 2021),
which in turn triggered a DNR moratorium on drilling and recompletion permits
throughout the Sulphur Mines Field.  Plaintiffs allege they have suffered
significant damages from their inability to develop the field.

Multiple defendants are insurance companies named as excess or CGL carriers for
various operators.  Lonquist & Company and Lonquist Field Service are also
defendants.

V.   THE SULPHUR MINES FIELD
================================================================================

Located in Calcasieu Parish, Louisiana.  A heavily developed oil and gas field
around a major salt dome.  Over one thousand wells have been drilled in the
field historically.  Yellow Rock's lease acreage covered approximately 5,000
acres around the dome in all directions.

White Top and Yellow Rock identified 71 ranked drilling prospects plus 22
additional geoscience potential locations (A through V) throughout the field.
All quadrants of the dome were represented.

White Top drilled four new wells and performed several recompletions of legacy
wells during Easley's management tenure.

The new management team (post-August 2021) drilled approximately one well
(Fee 1031, completed as a commercial wet well with modest production) and
performed some recompletions before the DNR permit moratorium halted all
development in late 2022/early 2023.

VI.  KEY TESTIMONY SUBJECTS
================================================================================

A. The Pressure Loss Event
   - Easley was not involved in day-to-day operations at the time (was on the
     farm).  First informed by Jonathan Love in approximately Q1 2023.
   - His layman's understanding: a massive leak in a cavern requiring
     injection of salt water to offset pressure losses.
   - He disputes that the timing was specifically December 2021 as a start
     date; he says he does not know when the occurrence began.
   - In his personal view, the event appears to have had a permanent impact on
     the field's developability; a six-foot-tall levee was built around the
     field and no foreseeable permits are being issued.

B. Theories as to Cause
   - Theory 1 (SWD wells): Yellow Rock's S4 and S7 salt water disposal wells
     injected too much water near the salt, possibly dissolving it from outside.
     Easley says this theory was, in his view, put to rest after the December
     2019 DNR meeting with Commissioner Ieyoub.
   - Theory 2 (Drilling too close): A directional oil and gas well may have
     been drilled too close to the salt or penetrated it.
   - Easley's personal opinion: he believes Yellow Rock's operations had no
     impact on the pressure loss event.

C. DNR Regulatory History
   - Yellow Rock used Jo Beth Taylor of KJ Consulting (Lafayette) as its
     regulatory consultant for direct interface with DNR.
   - Stephen Lee was the manager/department head of DNR's Mines & Minerals
     Division overseeing all Louisiana salt dome cavern operations.
   - December 2019: Formal meeting in Baton Rouge attended by Westlake and
     others, including Commissioner Ieyoub.  Issues: S4 and S7 SWD wells;
     DNR threatened public notice to require plug and abandonment.  Yellow Rock
     voluntarily curtailed injection volumes; regulatory action placed on hold.
   - February 2019: Lee informally told Yellow Rock the 1316 well was "too
     close to the edge of the dome" for SWD conversion.  1,320-foot minimum
     setback from dome edge -- informal policy, not in written rules.
   - Westlake itself had a permit request pending with DNR for approximately
     three years as of April 2019 (as noted by Easley in an e-mail -- Easley
     characterizes the DNR as taking longer than typical for oil and gas wells
     near the salt dome structure).

D. Financial History
   - Production declined from ~409 barrels/day (March 2020) to ~140 BOE/day
     (January 2021) to ~70 barrels/day (December 2021) -- dramatically
     impacted by Hurricane Laura (August 2020), Hurricane Delta, and tropical
     storms, which shut in the field and destroyed two power stations.
   - Loan from Sunny Solar matured January 29, 2021; White Top could not repay.
   - January 2021: generating ~$20,000/month positive cash flow before debt
     service and principal repayment.
   - Required $6 million in capital investment to reach 1,000 BOE/day.
   - Payroll taxes withheld from employees but not paid to government as of
     December 2021 (hundreds of thousands of dollars).
   - No LLC member had recouped their investment as of December 2021 or as of
     the date of this deposition.

E. August 2021 Restructuring
   - White Top explored Chapter 11 bankruptcy (hired Dick Fuqua, bankruptcy
     attorney) but ultimately executed a negotiated restructuring with Sunny
     Solar rather than filing.
   - Sunny Solar took majority control; Easley and French replaced as managers.
   - Jonathan Barrett became chief executive; Jonathan Love became COO.
   - Matt Steele brought in to assemble and oversee new technical team.

F. Criticism by Matt Steele
   - Matt Steele (chemical engineer retained by Barrett) characterized
     Easley's team's record-keeping as "amateurish" and used the word
     "ineptitude."
   - Easley disputes these characterizations.  He attributes shortcomings to
     limited resources, constrained budget, and operating in a difficult
     environment (COVID, hurricanes, capital drought).
   - He acknowledges the new team was "much more intense and rigorous" and had
     more resources, but he does not consider his team's work product the
     result of ineptitude.  As a CPA, he notes he was more qualified than
     Steele in financial/accounting matters; Steele was more qualified as a
     chemical engineer.

G. Cavern Proximity and Drilling Safety
   - Yellow Rock established a practice of circulating proposed well
     coordinates to Boardwalk and Westlake to coordinate and obtain their
     concurrence before drilling.  Obtained concurrence in all instances.
   - Todd Bertelot of Boardwalk (Vonco) provided Yellow Rock with 3D cavern
     visualization data (Voxler software) showing subsurface cavern locations
     relative to proposed drill sites.
   - Bertelot's April 2019 assessment: drilling with a southerly directional
     component from the 1014 location, the BLM No. 4 cavern was "a nothing
     burger."
   - Easley testified he was aware proximity to the salt dome edge was a known
     risk factor for drilling (increased risk of stuck pipe, cost overruns).
   - Yellow Rock produced a revised drill schedule (Exhibit 155) listing 71
     ranked prospects and 22 additional geoscience locations distributed in all
     quadrants of the dome.

H. Seismic Data
   - White Top conducted a 3D seismic survey via White Top Oil and Gas LLC.
   - Data was offered/pitch to investors as a quality differentiator.
   - Seismic data was later reprocessed by new management team.
   - Dorothea Faw (geophysicist with Indepth Geophysical) and Richard Lofton
     were retained for bright spot analysis; Easley was concerned they were
     attempting to expand scope to a more extensive costly reprocessing
     engagement.

I. Investor/Fiduciary Issues
   - Easley denies hiding any material information from investors or lenders.
   - He understands fiduciary duty as obligation to fair dealings, honesty, and
     disclosure to LLC members and lenders.
   - He states that materiality and permanence matter for disclosure
     obligations; the pressure loss event, in his current view, appears
     permanent given the ensuing DNR moratorium.
   - He does not know whether the pressure loss event was formally disclosed
     to investors before the lawsuit was filed.
