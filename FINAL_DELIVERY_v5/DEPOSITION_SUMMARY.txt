INCIDENT / CLAIMS OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The witness's general understanding of the case:
    Yellow Rock alleges it has been unable to obtain DNR permits to re-complete
    existing wells or drill new wells in the Sulphur Mines salt dome field.
    Yellow Rock attributes this inability to Westlake's alleged mismanagement
    of its salt cavern operations, specifically the loss of pressure in
    Westlake cavern 6 or 7 beginning in the December 2021 timeframe.

The Pressure Loss Event (as understood by witness):
    Westlake cavern 6 or 7 experienced a significant pressure loss requiring
    massive injection of salt water to offset the loss. Witness characterizes
    this as a "leak." Witness learned of the event in the first quarter of
    2023 through Jonathan Love and subsequent board briefings.

Theories heard by witness regarding cause:
    1. Yellow Rock's salt water disposal wells (S4 and S7) injected too much
       water near the salt dome, potentially dissolving salt from outside.
    2. A drilling operation penetrated or drilled too close to the salt dome,
       potentially piercing a cavern from outside.
    Witness personally believes Yellow Rock's SWD wells had no impact on the
    pressure loss event, citing proper regulatory operation of those wells.

Regulatory History:
    - November/December 2019: Formal meeting in Baton Rouge with DNR
      Commissioner Ieyoub regarding Yellow Rock's S4 and S7 SWD wells.
      Yellow Rock voluntarily curtailed injection to avoid mandatory plug
      and abandonment.
    - February 2019: DNR/Stephen Lee rejected proposal to convert Fee 1016
      to a SWD well as "too close to the edge of the dome" (less than
      1,320 feet from dome edge).
    - April 2019: Yellow Rock confirmed that DNR would not issue any drilling
      permit in the Sulphur Mines area without sign-off from Injection and
      Mining Division (IMD/Stephen Lee), and that IMD required no technical
      objections from Boardwalk or Westlake before approving any permit.
    - December 2022/January 2023: DNR stopped issuing drilling or recompletion
      permits; operations effectively halted.

KEY TESTIMONY POINTS (5–7 BULLETS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. REGULATORY DEPENDENCY: By April 2019, Yellow Rock knew that no drilling
   permit would be issued in the Sulphur Mines area without sign-off from
   DNR's Injection and Mining Division, and that IMD in turn required no
   technical objections from Boardwalk or Westlake. This created a de facto
   veto for cavern operators over Yellow Rock's drilling program.

2. CAVERN PROXIMITY AWARENESS: In April 2019, Easley actively sought 3D
   subsurface visualization data from Todd Bertelot/Boardwalk (Voxler software)
   to map cavern locations before finalizing pad 1014's drilling direction. John
   Miller's January 2019 meeting notes described prospect CCA as "higher risk
   due to being directly up against the salt edge." DNR's 1,320-foot setback
   rule for SWD wells was discussed with Easley at the time.

3. FINANCIAL DISTRESS PREDATING PRESSURE LOSS EVENT: Yellow Rock was in severe
   financial distress throughout 2020-2021 — Hurricane Laura devastated the
   field, production dropped from 409 to 70 BBL/day, White Top defaulted on its
   Sunny Solar loan, unpaid payroll taxes reached ~$500K, and no D&O insurance
   was in place. This financial distress was entirely independent of the pressure
   loss event.

4. MANAGEMENT TRANSITION AND STEELE CRITICISM: Matt Steele, retained by Barrett
   to evaluate the field, characterized prior management's record-keeping as
   "amateurish" and used the word "ineptitude" in describing their work. Easley
   did not agree with this characterization, attributing shortfalls to limited
   budget and personnel constraints.

5. WITNESS DISCLAIMS ENGINEERING EXPERTISE: Easley explicitly acknowledged he
   is not a licensed engineer, not a petroleum engineer, and not a geologist.
   His oversight role was administrative, financial, and managerial. Technical
   decisions were made by geologists (Wipple), geophysicists, and engineers
   (Brandl). All opinions regarding drilling safety were explicitly labeled by
   witness as "personal belief" or "personal opinion."

6. PRESSURE LOSS THEORIES: Witness heard two theories — salt water injection
   wells and drilling proximity to caverns. He personally dismissed both
   theories as having caused the pressure loss, but acknowledged the event
   appears to have resulted in a "permanent" moratorium on development with a
   six-foot levee surrounding the field, effectively destroying the field's
   development value.

7. DEPOSITION TERMINATED BY PLAINTIFF'S COUNSEL: After approximately 5 hours
   33 minutes on record, plaintiff's counsel terminated the deposition over
   Westlake counsel's objection. Westlake stated on the record that it was
   prepared to continue. Darren Guillot (Starr Indemnity) reserved right to
   re-depose the witness.
