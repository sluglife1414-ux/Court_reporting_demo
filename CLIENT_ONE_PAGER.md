## What We Do

You take a rough draft from your steno machine and turn it into a finished, delivered transcript. That process — the cleanup, the formatting, the double-checks — takes hours. Hours you are not billing for.

We built an engine that does that work before it reaches you.

Not a generic grammar checker. Not spell-check with a law dictionary. An engine that learns **your** style — and applies it to every transcript before you open it.

---

## How It Works

**Step 1 — You send us four things (once):**

| What | Why |
|------|-----|
| One finished transcript you have already delivered | We learn your style from your own work |
| Your RTF file from CaseCATalyst | The engine reads steno output natively |
| Your technical word list or dictionary (optional) | Medical terms, proper names, industry vocab |
| A short style form (10 minutes) | Catches the details a single sample might miss |

**Step 2 — We build your profile.**

We map how you format Q and A. How you handle objections. Your punctuation preferences. Your state's format requirements. Everything that makes your transcripts look like *yours* — not a generic template.

This happens once. You never fill out a form again.

**Step 3 — Every depo after that:**

You send us the RTF. If you recorded audio, send that too.

We return a pre-cleaned transcript — formatted to your style, your state, your standards. Steno errors corrected. Names and numbers cross-checked. Anything uncertain is flagged for your review, not silently changed.

You open it. You review it. You sign it.

---

## What the Engine Never Does

**It never touches what the witness said.**

If the witness said it under oath, that is the sworn record. Even if it is grammatically wrong. Even if the steno machine almost certainly misheard it — if we are not certain, we flag it for you. You decide. Always.

Your name goes on the transcript. That does not change.
